About me

I grew up in the Flint Hills of Kansas, and still love the open sky, wide expanses of treeless prairie, and the constant Kansas wind.   

I did my undergraduate work at Kansas State University, where I initially wanted to go into veterinary medicine, changing my mind at the last semester to go to graduate school.

I did my master's work at Fort Hays State University with Rob Channell, who introduced me to the wonderful world of macroecology, which led me to become a Weecologist in the White lab at Utah State University, where I am a member of the Biology Department and the Ecology Center.  

I believe that science can advance most quickly when researchers are willing to freely share data and ideas and am a major proponent of open access to data.  


I was diagnosed with fibromyalgia in December 2013, after a certain amount of struggle to figure out what was going wrong in the first place.  The story of how that has affected my ability to do research, and how I have adapted can be found here:
